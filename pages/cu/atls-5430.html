ATLS 5430

Final Project

AMBIESENSE

Design a prototype (product, device, tool, etc.) for a pandemic (past, present, or future). Potential research or problem areas: physical health, telemedicine, social distancing, social connection, mental health, self-care, remote education, pandemic prevention, quarantine solutions, food or resource delivery, humor, therapy, etc.

AMBIESENSE was designed as a mobile app prototype to help mental health during a pandemic (specifically, when quarantined). It allows individuals to project new and calming atmospheres, allow social interactions, or mimic the feelign of travel without leaving home.

Documentation

Figma, Airtable, Illustrator, Dimension, Premiere

Design Challenges

Design Challenge 1: Lab Logo

Create an ATLAS Studio/Lab logotype (the abstracted "A") for your design studio/lab that reflects your interests and career goals. Draw this by hand or design it in a software of your choosing, but your solution should be black and white and resonate with the existing ATLAS Lab branding.

This lowercase "a" was designed to break the pattern of using only capital letters for ATLAS Labs.

Illustrator, Premiere

Design Challenge 2: Origin Story

Write and illustrate a unique origin story. Think about the deeper meanings embedded in the narrative.

The goal of this origin story was to represent the birth of a hero or villain without the use of words. It uses a minimalist design and over-exaggerated expressions to satirize the stereotypical tragic backstory of many popular characters.

Documentation

Illustrator, InDesign, Premiere

Design Challenge 3: Chindogu

Design a seemingly useful object that addresses an everyday problem or opportunity. The problem/opportunity must be in your lived environment. You can only use found materials and objects (you cannot spend money on this project) to construct a prototype.

The designed Chindogu solves two household issues simultaneously: the desire for airflow and cooling, and the entertainment of a cat while left alone.

Premiere

Design Challenge 4: Slowness

Design a prototype that encourages mindfulness, meditation, self-awareness, contemplation, etc. The prototype can be digital, analog, or a combination of the two.

This digital prototype synchronizes a 10-second loop of falling water streams in a calming light environment to promote meditation and lower anxiety.

Illustrator, p5.js, Premiere

Design Challenge 5: Error

Create a prototype that integrates error, or "misuse" in a positive or interesting way. You can make the prototype or it can be a conceptual sketch or drawing.

The designed prototype interactively shows the proper use of fume hoods through a visual display that responds to the height of the sash. The visual display anthropomorphizes the fume hood via common human facial expressions, providing empathetic feedback for proper and improper use.

p5.js, Premiere

Design Challenge 6: Drawing Machine

Create a machine—analog, digital, or a hybrid of the two—that draws, or allows a user to draw through an unconventional interface.

This unconventional drawing machine takes in audio (pre-recorded) and visual represents all natural tones each frame in a circular pattern. This allows the visual analysis of different genres and periods of music.

p5.js, Premiere

Design Challenge 7: Regenerative Design

Design and describe a Regenerative Design Process. Regenerative Design is about designing to go beyond sustainability. Instead of maintaining the status quo, it improves, restores, re-nourishes, and makes better. Think about regenerative braking in a car: we harness and reuse the energy that is typically lost when slowing down a car. Same thing with compost: what was considered waste becomes food, enriching the soil.

The concept behind my regenerative design is to restore individual energy through a time management system. Allowing interspersed breaks after shorter periods of work (determined for each individual) has been proven to increase productivity during those work periods.

Documentation

Premiere

Design Challenge 8: Speculative Design Prop

Create a speculative prop that addresses the following (chosen) scenario: food supply, contopia and far future (100+ years).

The speculative prop "Supot" represents the extreme effort of sustainably providing nourishment to the world at that cost of losing the language of food.

Documentation

Illustrator

Design Challenge 9: Pandemic Design Probe

Design and develop a Design Probe for a specific test group: people without homes. The goal of the probe is to get feedback on people's experience during the pandemic.

The cultural probe "Probe in a Box" was designed not only to gather information of the experience of homeless people during the pandemic, but also to provide assistance and tools for transitioning from homelessness.

Documentation

InDesign, Illustrator
